Algoshift

Field: Labor Economics | JEL Codes: J81, J88

Definition

Labor scheduling where algorithms dynamically adjust worker hours based on real-time demand predictions, creating unpredictability and externalizing scheduling costs onto workers.

Etymology

Compound blending "algorithmic" with "shift" (work period). The term captures both the technological mechanism (algorithm-driven) and the labor market outcome (shift schedule volatility).

Usage Example

Retail algoshift systems maximize firm efficiency while externalizing costs onto workers through unpredictable schedules that prevent secondary employment.

Implementation
